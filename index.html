Sean F. — GIS & Remote Sensing Portfolio

Fire Ecology Research Fellow (ORISE funding by USFS)

I developed GIS models to assess the potential impact of wildfires on recreational areas in Southern California’s National Forests. This was accomplished by creating scenario-based analyses under dry and moderate environmental conditions to identify high-risk recreational sites. This work supports forest management and safety planning.

I also designed Python, R, and Earth Engine tools to automate image downloading and fire detection analysis across Northern California.

Graduate Research Assistant, Cal Poly Humboldt

I analyzed phenology changes and their relation to biomass and drought-related tree mortality. Using Linux, VirtualBox, ENVI/IDL, and R, I produced cloud-free Landsat time series and derived phenological metrics. I integrated FIA data and ran multivariate stats. Thesis and manuscript in progress.
